I can get far too deep into almost anything. An F1 race starts with wondering who will win and ends with tire strategy and technical regulations. A song leads to its lyrics, live performances, and the story behind it. An idol variety show turns into questions about group dynamics, selection systems, and the industry around them.

> A path from robot arms to Nogizaka46 and then Max Verstappen looks fairly random. What connects it is the same question: how do different people, rules, and parts cooperate and compete until they produce something none of them could create alone?

## Core Professional Interests

### Control and Robotics

I do not dislike theory, but a page of equations always makes me ask, “So what happens when this actually moves?” Control is interesting because it forces mathematics to meet reality: a model can be elegant while a sensor remains noisy, and a stable simulation does not guarantee a cooperative machine. My current project threads are [2-DOF singularity analysis](../projects/2-dof-robot-arm/) and [cooperative agricultural robots](../projects/cooperative-agricultural-robots/).

### AI and Automation

My interest in AI is practical and a little skeptical. I follow ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Codex, AI agents, local language models, and LM Studio to learn where they genuinely help and where they can be confidently wrong. “Made with AI” is not an outcome by itself; saved time, verifiable work, and accountable decisions matter more.

### Computers and Mobility

I use or follow MATLAB, Python, ROS, C / C++, Git, GitHub, VS Code, Ubuntu, web technologies, computer hardware, semiconductor packaging, autonomous driving, electric vehicles, automotive control, and F1 engineering.

## Music and Japanese Idols

Western artists I regularly follow include Ed Sheeran, Taylor Swift, Eminem, Kendrick Lamar, Justin Bieber, SZA, Maroon 5, Sabrina Carpenter, Bruno Mars, and Billie Eilish. I also listen to Japanese pop, bands, idol music, anime songs, and film or drama soundtracks, including back number.

The idol groups I follow most are Nogizaka46, Hinatazaka46, and Sakurazaka46. Within Nogizaka46, I have especially followed Mao Ioki and Miku Ichinose. I am interested in member personalities, group relationships, variety-show interactions, selection systems, fan culture, and the structure of the idol industry.

## Anime

I keep watched, watchlist, and status-unconfirmed titles separate and do not assign ratings that I have not actually recorded.

:::details Preference Patterns
- **Sports and growth:** competition, training, teamwork, talent versus effort
- **Romance and youth:** character chemistry, gradual relationships, school life, group dynamics
- **Battle and worldbuilding:** power systems, moral conflict, sacrifice, organizations, long-term setup
- **Recurring theme:** how different personalities cooperate, compete, and change inside a group
:::

## Film, Games, and Digital Archiving

My film interests include science fiction, action, youth, psychology, social issues, coming-of-age stories, and soundtracks. Games are a secondary interest, especially Forza Horizon, racing, vehicle simulation, exploration, and collection systems.

I enjoy the moment when a pile of scattered material finally becomes searchable and reusable. I save articles and screenshots, extract and translate text, trace media back to its source, and turn loose links into Markdown or website content. The downside is that my collection speed often outruns my ability to absorb it. My tabs and watchlists carry the quiet confidence of things that know they will never be finished.
